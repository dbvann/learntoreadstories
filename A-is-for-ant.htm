Level 1 — First Words

A is for Ant

Practice one sound, read a few words, and enjoy one tiny Beach Cat story. Keep it light, happy, and short.

One sound A few words One tiny story Done
Picture idea: Beach Cat sniffs a tiny ant near an apple on the shore.

Today’s Lesson

  1. Say the sound
  2. Read the words
  3. Read the story
  4. Celebrate

Parent note

Beach Cat can be called Cat in the child-facing story. That keeps the words simple while still building the Beach Cat world.

Step 1 · 1 minute

Say the Sound

A

A can say /ă/ like ant.

Parent says: “Touch the letter A. Say /ă/. Now say ant.”

Step 2 · 3 minutes

Read a Few Words

Point to each card. Say the sound slowly, then read the word together.

anta-n-t
applea-pple
axa-x
ata-t
catc-a-t
sats-a-t
Before the story · Optional

Picture Talk

Parent says: “Beach Cat sees an ant. Beach Cat sees an apple. What do you think Cat will do?”

Fun future picture idea: the cat and the acrobat. The parent can say “acrobat,” but the child does not need to read that word yet.

Step 3 · 5 minutes

Tiny Story

Cat and the Ant

Cat sat.

Cat sat at the sea.

An ant ran.

Cat saw the ant.

Cat saw an apple.

“Ah,” said Cat.

Step 4 · 1 minute

Celebrate

If your child remembers the /ă/ sound, that is a win. Read the tiny story again only if it still feels fun.
