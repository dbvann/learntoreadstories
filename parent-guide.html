Parent Guide

You do not need to be a reading expert. This guide gives you simple, gentle ways to help your child read at home — without turning childhood into homework.

❤️ One sound. A few words. One tiny story.

The Simple Nightly Rhythm

Each lesson follows the same calm pattern. Your child hears one sound, reads a few words with that sound, meets those words in a tiny story, and stops before it feels like work.

👂 One Sound

Start with a sound your child can hear and repeat.

🔤 A Few Words

Practice a small set of words that use that sound.

📖 One Tiny Story

Read a short story where the words matter.

🌙 Done

End while it still feels good. Ten minutes is enough.

What to Say Tonight

Parents do not need a lecture. Sometimes they just need the words.

Start: “Tonight we’re listening for one sound: /b/.”
Try a few words: “Beach. Big. Bat. Boat. Do you hear /b/ at the beginning?”
Read the story: “Beach Cat saw a big boat.”
End: “That’s enough for tonight. You found the sound.”
